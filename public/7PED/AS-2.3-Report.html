    AS 2.3 Report

    Jun 13, 20243 min read

    • 7ped

    Report

    Intro

    Over the course of my training program, my goal was to improve my time from 00:27:23 min 4.67 km to less than 27 minutes 5 km. This goal had taken in account for what I had thought to be achievable for the given limitations on time, training ability and current fitness level. Before the running training, I had never properly trained for a running event or athletic/sport event for that matter. Therefore, the primary focus was on cardiovascular and respiratory performance as well as endurance in all areas. On my pre-test run, I found my limiting factor was my cardiovascular and respiratory systems.

    Body 1 Training Backgroud To Event

    For the six weeks of training I had available, I knew that my focus would have to be on the cardiovascular and respiratory side of things. This is because of my training background before this new sports goal. I up to this have been very heavy into my weight training, specifically muscular hypertrophy focused training. Hypertrophy training is a type of training focused on muscular growth, popularized by body-building. I had been training like this for over five to six months, highly consistently. This meant I had the flowing attributes: high muscular mass, relatively high muscular strength and time for training already built into my week. Using my knowledge of my sport’s outcome in a 5 km run, I knew I would have to make some physical changes. I will have to target muscular atrophy while sustaining muscular strength, improve cardiovascular efficiency and lung efficiency. Using this information, the most useful training for my situation would be continuous training, running. Throughout my training, I tried my best to keep a constantly high Rating of Perceived Exertion (R.P.E). This allowed times to be a good indicator of progress in my training. I found that when I de-loaded off my hypertrophy-based weights program that my times improved while (R.P.E) was consistent. This showed me that it was beneficial to my running goal. As well as lowered times i had sustained a fair amount of muscular atrophy I could tell this from weighing 85~86 kg to 80~79 kg by the end of my training. This is a chronic response, meaning it happened over a longer period. While the increase in performance was all so likely attributed to another chronic response, to improve cardiovascular & respiratory performance.

    Body 2 Injurys...

    The last few weeks of training I started to run into some issues with injury’s and winter sickness. By the end of the fourth week, I started to experience some pain in my shins after runs also around this time, I caught a minor cold. By the end of week 5 I couldn’t run without sharp pain or walk long distances. I found out shin splits get worse with impact so during week 5, to maintain my cardiovascular and respiratory fitness, I did use a stationary bike at the gym. As well as doing my best to maintain my fitness, I took spa’s every morning to try to aid in recovery. I used the spa throughout most of my training program, the actual effectiveness of this was not clear. But was certainty nice for the cold mornings but the break from running. The break allowed me to get another attempt at the park run where otherwise would not be possible.
